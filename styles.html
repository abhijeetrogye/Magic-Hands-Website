Bridal Mehndi

Elaborate and intricate henna designs applied on the hands and feet of the bride before her wedding day in South Asian cultures, symbolizing joy, beauty, and auspiciousness.

Arabic Mehndi

Characterized by bold, floral designs with larger empty spaces, typically applied using thick lines. It originated in Arabic countries and is known for its simplicity and elegance

Indo-Arabic

A blend of Indian and Arabic styles, featuring bold outlines and intricate fillings, often incorporating peacock motifs, paisleys, and geometric patterns.

Indian Mehndi

Known for its detailed and intricate designs covering the entire hands, forearms, feet, and shins. It includes motifs like paisleys, flowers, intricate lines, and dots, often extending up the arms and legs.
